What is this?

An opening repertoire explorer built from Lichess game data. Pick a side ("Playing as: White/Black") — that's your hero.

On your turn, moves are ranked by backed-up value: the score you'd get assuming you play optimally from here down the tree, while your opponent plays the popularity-weighted distribution of what real players actually play. The green-highlighted move is the recommended best line under that assumption.

On your opponent's turn, moves are shown in popularity order — what real players actually play, most common first. The position value reflects a popularity-weighted average over the opponent's top replies (the more popular the reply, the more it counts) — you need to be prepared for what they'll actually play, not just the engine-best move.

The 0.000–1.000 number is hero win-equivalent (draws count as 0.5). Moves with very small sample size (<50 games) are demoted to the bottom — noise from tiny samples shouldn't outrank real evidence.

Click any move to play it. Out-of-book positions link to the Lichess opening explorer.
